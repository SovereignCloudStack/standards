## Introduction

Cloud computing has become the de-facto standard for delivering compute to
and services to endcustomers. Datacenters and compute infrastructure have
so-called embodied emissions. Aside from the energy consumption at runtime
produced by the workload the embodied emissions are part of the overall
environmental impact.

In order to be able to judge the environmental impact of cloud workloads
to gain better understanding and find leverage to reduce energy consumption
a solid and transparent reporting mechanism is needed. Just as important is
comparability between environments - as such this reporting mechanism needs
to be standardized. This documents provides an architectural blueprint to
achieve this within SCC-compliant environments and describes the decisions
that were being taken.
